Emergency Call Handling Platform

Facilitating and streamlining the 911 call handling experience to deliver instructions and dispatch resources to callers.

Challenge

Become a product used on every call

Our client's goal is to let their product to be used on every call for service a 911 center receives. For this project, they want to build out an MVP and rethink the user experience with the goal of compelling their customers to use their product for all call types.

Get ready for the future

Besides solving the adoption issue, our client wants to increase their competence by being more adapted to handling calls beyond emergency calls and plan for what's next in emergency response beyond traditional call handling.

- PROBLEM STATEMENT -

How might we develop a solution that is widely adopted and used on every call with future-ready features and capabilities?



Process

Research

We started with conducting a UX Asessment of the current product. Next, based on our learnings, we conducted ten 1-hour user interviews with call handlers who currently use the platform and then synthesized the interviews to gain a better understanding. Here are some of the insights we gained:


Speed is the Key

For call handlers, what is on their minds is providing information to callers and dispatching the responders asap. Every second counts.

Multitasking and High Stress

Call handlers need to multitasking and thus are under high stress. This is in addition to being responsible for someone’s safety.


Need Flexibility

Emergency calls, especially police calls, differ widely in terms of severity and protocol. Call handlers cannot rely on a script of questions to ask.

Tracking Process

Much of the strain on call handlers is deciding what questions to ask next and knowing which questions have already been answered without being asked.


Workspace Breakdown

All of the participants use multiple screens in their workspace. On average they have 6.44 monitors. Thus, the new product we designed will need to be easy to browse and read through when surrounded by multiple monitors.

Ideation

Collaborative Workshop

To cap off the discovery phase of the engagement, we held a 2-day collaborative workshop with our client.

In addition to sharing research findings, we worked together to create personas, map out the current user journey, and build the ideal user journey.

Next, we discussed the feasibility and did Feature Prioritization to sort the features we plan to develop.

Based on these outcomes, we synthesized six Design Principles to guide our design process.

Design Principles

  • Supportive UI

    Reduce call handler’s cognitive load and provide timely feedback.

  • Streamline the Process

    Reduce the actions required and hide features and information that are not essential to the current task.

  • Easy to Find Features in Need

    Call handlers should be able to find the features they need as quick as possible.

  • Provide Flexibility

    Call handlers should use less structured way for gathering supportive information and be able to jump between multiple scripts freely.

  • High Readability and Scannability

    Call handlers need to work with multiple screens to go through a large amount of information in a timely manner.

  • Scalable and Customizable UI

    Take future features and modules, and the customization needs of different agencies into account.

    • Iteration

    Wireframes

    After we created the User Flow diagrams and the Information Architecture, we started the wireframe sprints. We initiated the sprint with designing the key screens and worked with our clients to finalize the design of them. We then created the flows and linked them into clickable prototypes in Figma to communicate our design decisions to our client.




    Visual Design

    When the wireframing phase is reaching to the end, we started to create visual concepts and to transit the wireframe library to visual design library by applying visual details.


    Deliverables

    Design Library File

    During the wireframe sprints, we created a components library in Figma as the design library that links to the wireframe file. After applying visual details to the components in the visual design phase, this file became the final design library including colors, fonts, icons, grids, shadows, and components with different states.

    In addition to the Design Library file, we delivered the Wireframe file and the Visual Design file with clickable prototypes and annotations on design details.



    Development File

    While we are wireframing the flows, we created a User Stories file to keep track of the features we designed and to ensure our client and the design team are on the same page.

    Based on this user stories file, we created a Product Requirements file with detailed descriptions of the features we designed and links to the figma files. This enables a smooth hand over from the design team to the development team.


    Client's Feedback

    We received 5 out of 5 rating from our client on clutch.co, a website for clients to rate the services they received from agencies and one of our major sources for clients.

    This is the first project our agency tried to combine the product design contract with a development contract. As the client is satisfied with the design work, they eventually agreed on extending the contract and hired us to develop this product.

Hi! I am Junxiang Yao,

a multidisciplinary UX/Product Designer specializing in interaction design, system thinking, and coding.

I have been a designer all my life. Started from Industrial Design, I then studied and practiced creating digital interactive experiences for more than 5 years. Now, I am able to work independently in all phases of product design, including research, strategy, ideating, prototyping, testing, and coding.

Currently, I'm working on redesigning and upgrading The Programming Foundation's website and learning platform. I focus on designing engaging learning experiences and helping spread coding literacy to unprivileged group, as I believe human-centered design is the answer to utilizing technology in the most empathetic and systematic way. Throughout 2020, I worked with The Children's Creativity Museum as a UX/UI Design Consultant working on creating a mobile portal to engage their current count of 10,000+ children and create a new revenue stream. I had the chance to serve as the design team's project manager and guided the team to navigate through the pandemic.

Prior to this, while pursuing my first Master's Degree from UC Santa Barbara, I worked on creating immersive interactive experience and information visualizations at The Experimental Visualization Lab as I am passionate about emergent interactive media. So, in my spare time, you can find me playing with code, headset, data, and browsers and exploring interaction possibilities in VR/AR/MR.

Challenge

CyberSense is a very feature rich and sophisticated tool, which is not easy for new users to use. As our client’s strategy has changed to provide services to regular users instead of experts, they need to simplify the user experience and minimize the workflows, so the product becomes easier to use by someone who has never used the product before. Thus, the three major objectives are:

  • Ease of Use

    The customers should be able to come in day one and understand what they’re supposed to do. It should be a tool that requires very little support after users receive their software license.

  • Innovation

    The client needs an interface that takes large amounts of data and makes it visually compelling, understandable and easy to navigate/dig into. This project should not just be a technology refresh, but also a redesign focused on the needs of users.

  • Confidence

    Confidence is the real value of CyberSense. The new user experience should be able to showcase data intelligently and incrementally shows that it’s working.

    • - PROBLEM STATEMENT -

    How might we redesign CyberSense to be intuitive for new users, while visualizing complex data in an intelligible and compelling way that builds user confidence?



    Discovery

    We worked with our clients in expert interviews to understand the use cases of CyberSense. We then conducted co-design sessions to specify user personas, current journey map, ideal journey map and "Now, Next , Backlog" for feature prioritization.



    Here are the main problems we identified:


  • There is no central place to see a whole picture. All the steps and information are scattered throughout various screens and applications.

  • What is currently available is hard for users to understand at a glance. The user really needs to hunt for the data and what to investigate.

  • These are not things that the user has to do often (such as installing CyberSense or analyzing an alert), so the user will often not remember how to do it.

    • Iteration

    Wireframes

    From the previous collaborations, we decided that Confifuratoin, Monitor, and Analyze are the features we will focus on. We worked with the client to create a document containing functions each feature should have and all the tasks related to them. From there, we started the wireframe sprints and created low-fidelity prototypes in Figma along the way.




    Visual Design

    We then created visual concepts and decided on the direction to transit the wireframes to high fidelity screens and clickable prototypes in Figma.





    Design Library

    A design library containing components link to the visual design file in Figma is also part of the final delivery, so that our client could evolve from current design with more features.


    Results

    Index Engines is satisfied with the design and thus extended the contract for us to further manage and support the development phase. This product successfully facilitated the growth of our client, they expanded their Software Engineering team with nearly 30 engineers, 8 months after the new dashboard design was announced.

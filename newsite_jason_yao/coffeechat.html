CoffeeChat

Jul. 2020

College students and recent college graduates need a way to find professionals in the field they are interested in for advice about career and life because of the mismatch between academia and industry. This app aims at facilitating the connection between students and professionals by providing a platform for students to ask for advice from the professionals who work in the industries they are interested in.

Advisor: Terri Rodriguez-Hong, Sam Kashani, Dana Ragouzous, Dana Lynn

Tools: Figma, Balsamiq, Keynote, MURAL, Miro

My Role: Solo Project (Some of the research data were used from this project.)

Duration: 2 months

The Challenge

Because of the mismatch between academia and industry, college students need practical advice about career and life to prepare for their graduation and start the career.

Many college students are facing a long and hard pathway to start their careers. When they graduate, it is typical for them to take some time to transition to the job market and find the position that leverage their skill and experience from school. Especially under COVID-19, students are facing a more complicated reality as their future became more uncertain.

  • 75% of HR professionals struggled in recruiting due to candidates’ lack of necessary skills.
  • 75% of students felt "I don't know what positions are a fit for me." in job search.
  • Due to COVID-19, 28% of students are seriously considering a career change/path.

How might we help college students/recent college graduates better identify and learn the "life skills" necessary in work that they may not have learned in school?

Solution

CoffeeChat aims at facilitating the connection between students and professionals. Students could use it to find professionals who have a similar background with them and currently works in the field/company they are interested in.

It digitalize the coffee chats between students and professionals for aforementioned purpose, so that students won't be hindered when they are seeking suggesions from the industry even under the circumstance of COVID-19.

Key Features

Call Booking

The users can search for consultants that fit their needs and check their information on their profile page. From there, they can book a video chat session for face to face consulting.

Question Asking

When the user has a simpler question they want to ask to a consultant, they can send a question to this consultant instead of booking a video call session. This is also a more flexible consulting approach when it is difficult to schedule a meeting time.

Pay Consultants with "Coffee"

Because this app aims to retain the coffee chat, which is a great way before pandemic for students to chat with professionals, the price in the app is shown as a coffee cup icon. Users can always change the price to be numeric by toggling the switch.

Research

Interviews

Qualitative interviews were conducted to understand how recent college graduates feel about their graduation and how they prepared for it, and to gather their thoughts about the education they received from college.
(Most of the research data were used from this project, which is a group project.)

"There's no one told me what I should do in life (before graduation). I searched online to see how other people dealt with similar problems before."

-- Eric, 22 years old, Industrial Designer, Graduated in May, 2019

"I feel like it's not enough to have a degree, and then you can solve those problems at work. A lot of time was spent on something that is not really mattering as much (in school.)"

-- Harly, 22 years old, Software Engineer, Graduated in May, 2019

"I struggled a lot when I was trying to find a job before graduation. I would like to help newer generations when they are starting their career. I don't want to earn money from it."

-- John, 28 years old, Backend Engineer, 3 years in industry

Insights

  • Recent college graduates don’t really understand what the choices they made really means when they were in school.
  • Many of them lacked timeline, guidance and direction for preparing for career.
  • There is a mismatch between what society need and what schools encourage. People need to realize the difference as soon as possible.
  • The mismatch between expectation and reality is very common for students.

Design Choices Reflection

Date&Time Picker

The user needs to pick the date and time from the consultants available times to schedule a video call sessioin. To reduce the steps to go through for booking, the challenge is visualizing the availability in a limited screen real estate.

Inspired by Google Calendar, my solution is, in one page, asking users to choose the duration, then to pick an ideal period of time, and to pick the start time of the call at the end. The iterations of this screen are shown on the right.

Replace Numeric Price with Coffee Cups

As the goal of the app is to bring the coffee chats to a digital format, it is important to tell users the consulting fees are at the price of cups of coffee. In addition, from the testing and critique sessions, I learned that showing this price strategy is effective to encourage new users to tryout this app. Thus, I used coffee cup icons to represent numeric price to keep the focus on experience and knowledge sharing, instead of money and profit.

The current solution is shown on the right. However, I am still looking for a better solution. I think more user testing sessions on this feature will bring insights about the directions to refine the current solution.

Future Steps

In my design, I have several hypotheses:

  1. Users prefer to see price shown as cups of coffee as CoffeeChat is trying to digitalize the coffee chats between students and professionals, which is not viable under pandemic.
  2. The information about consultants presented in each card and the profile page is enough. However, I will still try refining it by adding and removing content.
To test the hypothesis, I will conduct another round of user test with a higher fidelity of the prototype. Because Figma doesn't have code components, it cannot save the states of the prototype. For instance, it cannot really remember if the user had already booked a call with a specific consultant. I think this interferes with the testing results because the participants are not really regarding this prototype as realistic once they find out only several buttons in the prototype are clickable. They tend to rely on the indicators presented by Figma when they are not sure where to tap. I could use tools like Framer X to build a prototype with higher fidelity that can remember the state of the prototype.

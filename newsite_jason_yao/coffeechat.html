Challenge

Because of the mismatch between academia and industry, college students need practical advice to prepare for their careers.

Many college students are facing a long and hard pathway to start their careers. When they graduate, it is typical for them to take some time to transition to the job market and find the position that leverage their skill and experience from school. Under COVID-19, students are facing a more complicated reality as their future became more uncertain.


  • 75%

    of HR professionals struggled in recruiting due to candidates’ lack of necessary skills.

  • 75%

    of students felt "I don't know what positions are a fit for me." in job search.

  • 28%

    of students are seriously considering a career change/path due to COVID-19.

How might we help college students better identify and learn the practical skills necessary in work that they may not have learned in school?

Solution

CoffeeChat aims at facilitating the connection between students and professionals. Students could use it to find professionals who have a similar background and currently works in the field/company they are interested in.

It digitalize the coffee chats between students and professionals for experience and knowledge sharing, so that students won't be hindered when they are seeking suggesions from the industry even under the circumstance of COVID-19.

Video Chat Booking

Users can search for consultants that fit their needs and check their information on their profile page. From there, they can book a video chat session for face to face consulting.

Question-Based Consultation without Booking

When the user have more straightforward questions to ask a consultant, they can send an inquiry to this consultant instead of booking a video call session. This is also a more flexible consulting approach when it is difficult to schedule a meeting time.

Pay Consultants with "Coffee"

CoffeeChat uses coffee cups as credits to pay for the service as this app aims to retain and encourage the coffee chat, which is a great way for students to chat with professionals before the pandemic. The amount of payment will be decided by how much they use this platform.

Research

Interviews with Students

  • Qualitative interviews were conducted to understand college students' thoughts on college education and how they prepared for their careers.

  • "There's no one told me what I should do in life (before graduation). I searched online to see how other people dealt with similar problems before."

    -- Eric, Industrial Design Student

  • "If there are opportunities for mentorship, I want to know if they fit and understand my situation. I don’t want to waste their time and mine."

    -- Tom, Electronic Computer Engineering Student

Interviews with Professionals

  • I also interviewed prople who graduates within 3 years to learn their journey of starting their career and their thoughts on mentoring younger generations.

  • "I struggled a lot when I was trying to find a job before graduation. I would like to help newer generations when they are starting their career. I don't want to earn money from it."

    -- John, Backend Engineer, 3 years in industry

  • "If it takes a mentor too much commitment and time, I think it's fair for the mentee to offer something back."

    -- Sam, Software Engineer, 1 year in industry

Insights

  • College students lack timelines and directions for career preparation. It is hard to find experienced people who understand their situation.

  • In general, students don't want to pay too much for consultancy and mentorship. And if they need to pay more, it is essential to ensure the quality of the service.

  • It is controversial whether people should pay for mentorship relationships, and there is yet no standard price if payment is involved.

    • OPPORTUNITY #1

    How might we help students to consult professionals with ease？

    Market Research

    I studied how the current market solves this problem. There are two types of services, charged and free.

    In general, the price of the charged ones is kind of high for students. In MentorCruise, a 30-min introductory call can cost from $39 to $100.

    As for the free ones, like UX Coffee Hours, there are only a finite number of spots available, and they can be booked out fast. As a result, the coverage of current services is limited.

    OPPORTUNITY #2

    How might we design a service that could cover a larger group of students and professionals？

    Ideation

    OPPORTUNITY #1

    How might we help students to consult professionals with ease？

    To facilitate the process students consulting professionals, CoffeeChat uses video chat and text-based questions as the consulting channels.

    From my market research, all the services that tries to tackle this problem are based on video chatting for its accessibility. However, for the free ones, the slots can be booked out really fast; and for the charged ones, there is no alternative ways of consulting that is more affordable for students. Thus, to increase the flexibility and accessibility, CoffeeChat allows students to send consultants questions as text-based consultation.

    OPPORTUNITY #2

    How might we design a service that could cover a larger group of students and professionals？

    To attract professional to become consultants at CoffeeChat, incentives are important, as the professionals don’t have to use a platform to find mentees. Thus, consultants in this platform should be paid.

    However, not all students are willing to pay a lot for this type of service and not all professionals came to CoffeeChat for money. To facilitate and encourage coffee chats which comes from people’s good will, a flexible payment system is implemented with coffee cups as tokens.

    Students

    Coffee cups are used as tokens to pay for a limited number of consultations every month. If they want to book more sessions, they will need to pay for a higher price without using the tokens.

    Here are some of the rules:

    • Once students registered, there will be 8 coffee cups available for them to book calls.

    • Every week, the user can receive 1 cup for free until the number reached 10, which is the maximum.

    • Users can complete tasks (like watching ads) to earn coffee cups or purchase some directly.

    Consultants

    Consultants/Professionals have the freedom to set the number of call slots that can be paid with coffee cup tokens. The tokens they received will be shown in their data and be transferred as real money in their account.

    When the slots are all booked, they can still respond to several questions or set open consultation slots with higher prices. They can invest more time and open more high-price slots to earn more money.

    Iteration

    WireFrames

    I first sketched out the low fedelity wireframes on paper, and then used Figma to create mid-fidelity wireframes based on the sketches. Next, I used these wireframes to make a clickable mid-fidelity prototype in Figma for user testing.

    User Testing

    I conducted 6 rounds of remote user testing sessions because of the pandemic. The tool I used for testing are Zoom and Figma.
    Full Testing Script: click here.

    In synthesizing the data, I used miro to debrief all the test sessions on the virtual sticky notes in miro, and the sort them into themes based on four categories: observations, positive quotes, negative quotes, and errors. Next, I used Jakob Nielson’s usability scale to measure the intensity of the errors from 0 to 4, with 4 being a catastrophic usability issue.
    Testing Report: click here.

    Design Choices

    Date&Time Picker

    The user needs to pick the date and time from the consultants available times to schedule a video call sessioin. To reduce the steps to go through for booking, the challenge is visualizing the availability in a limited screen real estate.

    Inspired by Google Calendar, my solution is, in one page, asking users to choose the duration, then to pick an ideal period of time, and to pick the start time of the call at the end. The iterations of this screen are shown on the right.

    Replace Numeric Price with Coffee Cups

    As the goal of the app is to bring the coffee chats to a digital format, it is important to tell users the consulting fees are at the price of cups of coffee. In addition, from the testing and critique sessions, I learned that showing this price strategy is effective to encourage new users to tryout this app. Thus, I used coffee cup icons to represent numeric price to keep the focus on experience and knowledge sharing, instead of money and profit.

    The current solution is shown on the right. However, I am still looking for a better solution. I think more user testing sessions on this feature will bring insights about the directions to refine the current solution.

    Reflections

    In my design, I have several hypotheses:

    1. Users prefer to see price shown as cups of coffee as CoffeeChat is trying to digitalize the coffee chats between students and professionals, which is not viable under pandemic.
    2. The information about consultants presented in each card and the profile page is enough. However, I will still try refining it by adding and removing content.

    To test the hypothesis, I will conduct another round of user test with a higher fidelity of the prototype. Because Figma doesn't have code components, it cannot save the states of the prototype. For instance, it cannot really remember if the user had already booked a call with a specific consultant. I think this interferes with the testing results because the participants are not really regarding this prototype as realistic once they find out only several buttons in the prototype are clickable. They tend to rely on the indicators presented by Figma when they are not sure where to tap. I could use tools like Framer X to build a prototype with higher fidelity that can remember the state of the prototype.
